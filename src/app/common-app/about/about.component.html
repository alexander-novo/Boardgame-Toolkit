About the Project

Team 30 is designing a board game development toolkit and remote play environment for their project. The toolkit provides different assets to the users to develop their own style of a well-known game or something completely different with the twist of changing the rule set to provide players a fair advantage, as well as providing more accessible controls than other board-game simulators on the market. The toolkit will also allow players to create their own custom expansions or spin-offs for existing games. The remote play environment utilizes a uniqueness to operating board games with server-based in-browser multiplayer for the creator to invite players to their game with no downloads to the invited users to start playing and communicating immediately.

Project Domain Resources

[1] Matthew Berland and Victor R. Lee. “Collaborative Strategic Board Games as a Site for Distributed Computational Thinking”. In: International Journal of Game-Based Learning 1.2 (Apr. 2011), pp. 65–81. doi:10.4018/ijgbl.2011040105. A journal article on the power of computational thinking in cooperative board games. Goes in depthabout the difference in computational thinking in humans who play cooperative board games versus humans who play competitive board games, due to spreading thinking across multiple people. Results in humans could extend in an interesting way to computer players playing with other humans, which historically have been bad at predicting human behaviours.

[2] Renee Garett et al. “A Literature Review: Website Design and User Engagement”. In: Online journal of communication and media technologies 6.3 (2016), pp. 1–14.doi:10.29333/ojcmt/2556. A journal article about web design and common elements thought to be important in user engage-ment. Elements include organization, content utility, navigation, graphical representation, purpose,and simplicity. Useful for designing a web app with a large amount of user engagement.

[3] F. Gobet, J. Retschitzki, and A. de Voogt.Moves in Mind: The Psychology of Board Games. Taylor & Francis, 2004. isbn: 9781135425135. url: https://books.google.com/books?id=hfF5AgAAQBAJ. An overview of the psychology of playing and designing board games. Includes strategies to play boardgames, effects on cognisance, and how to learn to play board games effectively. Useful for figuring outhow people attack learning to play board games for UI, Rules API, and AI design purposes.

[4] Vincent Hom and Joe Marks. “Automatic design of balanced board games”. In: Proceedings of the AAAIConference on Artificial Intelligence and Interactive Digital Entertainment (AIIDE). 2007, pp. 25–30. An article on using a Genetic Algorithm (GA) to design balanced board games. This algorithm could be used to help make suggestions to potential board game designers to help balance their games.The algorithm makes heavy use of a “General Game Playing” algorithm, however our rules systemgenerating action lists could be used instead.

[5] Board Game Design Lab.Tools and Resources. 2020. url: https://www.boardgamedesignlab.com/ (visited on 10/19/2020). A website full of tools for board game designers, including tutorials, free assets, prototyping tools, and more. Hopefully our toolkit will one day be on this website. Includes a list of competitors and assets which our tool should be able to support.

[6] M. Selinker et al.The Kobold Guide to Board Game Design. Open Design LLC, 2012. isbn: 9781936781041. url: https://books.google.com/books?id=S7rimAEACAAJ. A book about board game design. Includes many different mechanics and rules templates, perfect forunderstanding the capabilities of our future Rules API. Also includes chapters about asset design and creation, for the purposes of knowing what kind of assets we will need to include and region creation.

[7] W3schools.Node.js Introduction. 2020. url: https://www.w3schools.com/nodejs/nodejs_intro.asp (visited on 10/19/2020). An introduction to NodeJS - a popular programming languages for managing web servers and RESTAPIs. Will be used for managing both the Project Storage System and the Rules System.
