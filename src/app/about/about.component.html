Board Game Development Toolkit

This is our Senior Project made within UNR, CSE Department for CS 426 during Spring 2021 semester

This is created by Team 30 within the class consisting of Ryan Keeler, Casey Lamb, Anthony Novotny, Tyler Stewart

Eelke Folmer (Advisor), David Feil-Seifer (Instructor), Devrin Lee (Instructor)

About the Project: Team 30 is designing a board game development toolkit and remote play environment for their project. The toolkit provides different assets to the users to develop their own style of a well-known game or something completely different with the twist of changing the rule set to provide players a fair advantage, as well as providing more accessible controls than other board-game simulators on the market. The toolkit will also allow players to create their own custom expansions or spin-offs for existing games. The remote play environment utilizes a uniqueness to operating board games with server-based in-browser multiplayer for the creator to invite players to their game with no downloads to the invited users to start playing and communicating immediately.
